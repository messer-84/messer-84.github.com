NEW!

 

 

We are happy to announce that Sphere will be launching its first formal, annual Performance Review Cycle via Chairlift during the week of December 3rd, 2018.


The purpose of this new, annual format is to:

  1. Standardize the performance review process for all Sphere employees at all locations
  2. Automate the process
  3. Link individual performance to standard annual merit review and increase

Here is how it works:


Year End Reviews will be scored on a 5 point scale in the following categories:

  • Expertise 25%
  • Communication, Collaboration, and Interpersonal Skills 15%
  • Problem Solving 25%
  • Leadership Behaviors 30%
  • Company Values 5%

Rating Scale:

  • 0.0 - 2.6 = Does Not Meet Expectations
  • 2.7 - 2.9 = Does Not Meet Some Expectations
  • 3.0 - 3.7 = Meets Expectations
  • 3.8 - 4.8 = Exceeds Most Expectations
  • 4.9 - 5.0 = Exceeds All Expectations

  • Each employee will receive a weighted score for each category and an overall weighted average review score.
  • Self-evaluations and feedback from clients or project managers will be taken into consideration.
  • Each employee’s overall weighted average score will affect the percent of merit increase target the employee will receive.
  • If an employee received an increase to their salary throughout the year, the difference will be reflected in the maximum target they will be eligible to receive at year end.
  • To be eligible for the annual merit increase, you must be employed with Sphere for at least three months.
  • If you are employed less than three months, you will still receive a performance evaluation, which will be counted as your end of trial period evaluation.
  • All merit increases are prorated based on the number of months of employment at the time of the annual review cycle.

Once your performance review has been finalized, you will be able to view the scores and commentary in Chairlift. You will also have a 1:1 meeting with your HR Manager during the month of January to discuss the results of your review and any potential changes to your salary.


Merit increases will be effective with the first paycheck of 2019.


Should you have any questions, please don’t hesitate to contact your local HR Manager.
